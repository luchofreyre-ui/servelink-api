## What This Is

General soil on finished wood shows as dulled traffic lanes, faint heel marks, and embedded grit that whispers under shoes.

## Why It Happens

Grit acts like sandpaper under pet nails and rolling chairs; kitchen-adjacent wood sees finer oily film.

## What People Do Wrong

People wet-mop like tile, use steam against manufacturer guidance, or drag stiff brushes across bevels.

## Professional Method

Vacuum or dry microfiber first, damp flat mop with manufacturer-approved cleaner sparingly, and detail edges without puddling at seams.

## Data and Benchmarks

Traffic lane contrast often exceeds absolute soil thickness—lighting angle controls perceived severity.

## Professional Insights

Area rugs without pad can trap grit at edges—lift and detail periodically.

## When to Call a Professional

Call a professional when recoating intervals arrive, when finish type is uncertain, or when cupping suggests moisture—not soil alone.
