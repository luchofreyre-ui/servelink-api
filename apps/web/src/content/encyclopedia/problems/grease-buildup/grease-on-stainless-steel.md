## What This Is

Stainless steel in kitchens shows grease as smudges, directional streaks, and dull patches that follow the grain of the finish.

## Why It Happens

Cooking aerosols and hand oils adhere strongly; brushed grain makes uneven residue highly visible under side lighting.

## What People Do Wrong

Wiping against the grain, over-wetting electronics-adjacent panels, or leaving cleaner residue that reads as new streaks.

## Professional Method

Dry dust first, apply light degreaser or stainless-appropriate cleaner, wipe with the grain in long passes, then dry-buff with clean microfiber.

## Data and Benchmarks

Residue control matters more than product brand; excess liquid is a common cause of poor appearance.

## Professional Insights

Treat hoods and vertical fridge panels as finish-sensitive; control product volume strictly.

## When to Call a Professional

Call when coatings are specialty black stainless or when pitting or discoloration suggests damage, not soil.
