## What This Is

Streak-free glass cleaning is a controlled finishing method used on mirrors, windows, shower glass, and glass furniture. The goal is to remove soil and residue without leaving haze, drag marks, or overspray trails.

## Why It Happens

Glass shows residue quickly because it is highly reflective and often receives direct light. Product overuse, dirty towels, and poor drying technique all contribute to visible streaking.

## What People Do Wrong

Most people use too much cleaner, use dirty towels, clean in direct sun, or fail to separate soil removal from dry finishing. They also treat mineral deposits like routine glass soil, which creates frustration and repeated rewiping.

## Professional Method

Assess whether the problem is routine film or mineral residue. Use a low-residue cleaner for routine glass, apply minimally, wipe with a clean microfiber, then dry-finish using a dedicated dry side or towel. Work edges carefully.

## Data and Benchmarks

Glass clarity depends heavily on towel quality, product residue control, and drying technique. On routine glass, technique often matters more than product brand.

## Professional Insights

Streak-free results usually come from restraint, not saturation. The cleaner should assist the towel, not flood the surface.

## When to Call a Professional

Call a professional when haze persists after proper glass cleaning, when shower glass may be etched, or when large glass areas require restoration-quality results.
